# Reyes Holdings – Driver Risk & Fleet Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview
This project analyzes driver safety, fleet operations, and risk patterns across multiple divisions of Reyes Holdings using an interactive analytics dashboard. The goal of this analysis is to identify operational risk, monitor driver behavior, and support data-driven decision making for fleet safety management.

The dashboard provides insights into driver distribution, vehicle usage, risk categories, and coachable safety events across different divisions and geographic regions.

## Objectives
- Analyze driver safety performance across divisions
- Identify high-risk driving patterns and coachable safety events
- Monitor vehicle usage and fleet distribution
- Support operational safety improvements through data insights
- Provide executives with visual analytics for risk monitoring

## Tools & Technologies
- Power BI (Dashboard Development)
- Data Visualization
- Data Analysis
- Business Intelligence
- Fleet Safety Analytics

## Dashboard Features

### 1. Driver & Fleet Overview
The dashboard displays the total number of drivers across the organization and tracks fleet distribution by vehicle type.

Key vehicles analyzed include:
- Tractor Trailer
- Delivery Trucks
- Standard Vans
- Pickup Trucks
- Mini Vans

Tractor trailers represent the largest portion of the fleet operations.

### 2. Geographic Risk Distribution
A geographic map visualizes the distribution of fleet operations and risk signals across the United States.

Key operational locations include:
- Fairfield, OH
- California
- Baton Rouge, LA
- Manassas, VA
- Stockton, CA
- McCook
- Florida

This allows stakeholders to quickly identify operational hotspots and risk-heavy regions.

### 3. Company Division Analysis
The dashboard compares performance across four major divisions:

- Company A
- Company B
- Company C
- Company D

Company A accounts for the majority of activity (~56.7%), followed by Company B (~25.36%), Company C (~10.30%), and Company D (~7.64%).

### 4. Risk Category Analysis
Driver activity is categorized into three risk levels:

- Low Risk
- Medium Risk
- High Risk

Summary of coachable events:
- Low Risk: 34,464
- Medium Risk: 44,826
- High Risk: 2,418
- Total Coachable Events: 81,708

The majority of activity falls under **medium and low risk categories**, indicating opportunities for safety coaching and driver improvement programs.

### 5. Coachable Event Trends
The dashboard tracks coachable safety events by month and quarter.

Time range covered:
- Q3 2023 to Q3 2024

The analysis reveals fluctuations in safety incidents across months, allowing fleet managers to identify seasonal trends and operational risks.

### 6. Division-Level Safety Performance
Different operational divisions show varying levels of coachable safety events. This enables leadership to identify which divisions require additional safety training or operational improvements.

## Key Business Insights
- Company A contributes the largest portion of fleet operations and safety events.
- Medium-risk driver behavior represents the largest category of coachable incidents.
- Certain operational regions show higher driver activity and potential safety exposure.
- Monthly event tracking enables proactive safety monitoring and coaching opportunities.

## Business Recommendations
- Implement targeted safety coaching for medium-risk drivers.
- Focus safety training initiatives on divisions with higher coachable events.
- Introduce predictive risk monitoring using driver behavioral analytics.
- Deploy regional safety programs in high-activity operational locations.
- Monitor seasonal safety patterns to improve fleet management planning.

## Project Value
This dashboard demonstrates how business intelligence tools can transform operational safety data into actionable insights. By visualizing driver risk behavior and fleet performance, organizations can proactively improve driver safety, reduce operational risk, and enhance fleet management strategies.
